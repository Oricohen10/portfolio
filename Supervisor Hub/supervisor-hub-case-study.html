The Moment

The decision
the AI
couldn't make.

When a policy crosses $500K, Verint's Agentic AI stops. The decision is too significant, the liability too real. The supervisor gets 3 minutes - and until now, no dedicated place for that moment to land.

Supervisor Hub is that place. Not a notification. An interruption that demands a response.

Interrupt Modal screenshot The red modal arriving on the supervisor's screen mid-shift
The Problem

The AI can decide most things.
But not everything. And that gap is expensive.

Contact center supervisors manage 8-15 agents simultaneously. When the AI hits a threshold it can't cross alone, the supervisor must step in - with no dedicated tool, no immediate context, and no structured way to document the decision.

🔔
No dedicated interrupt
High-stakes requests arrived as generic alerts buried among routine notifications. A $600K decision competed for attention with shift updates.
🧩
No context at the moment of decision
Supervisors had to navigate multiple systems to gather customer info, risk scores, and policy details before they could make a call.
📋
No audit trail
Decisions were made verbally or via chat. No rationale, no timestamp, no compliance-ready documentation. A liability waiting to happen.
The Flow

From trigger
to closed case.
Under 3 minutes.

Every step is designed around one constraint: the supervisor must make a confident, documented decision - as fast as possible, without cutting corners on compliance.

1
AI Agent reaches its limit
Policy exceeds the $500K automated threshold. The AI creates an authorization case and hands off to the supervisor.
2
Supervisor is interrupted
A full-screen modal takes over. Customer name, policy amount, risk score, SLA countdown. It can't be dismissed without acknowledgment.
3
Case is reviewed
All context pre-assembled: policy details, compliance checks, AI analysis, comparable case rates. No system-switching required.
4
Decision + rationale submitted
Approve, Reject, or Request Documents. Every decision requires a rationale - Copilot drafts it, the supervisor reviews and submits.

Screen 03 · Decision + Rationale

The supervisor decides.
The Copilot explains why.

Every Approve or Reject requires a rationale. The Copilot prepares a draft based on the AI analysis - accurate, editable, and ready to submit. The supervisor owns the decision; the Copilot reduces the cognitive load of documenting it.

This friction is intentional. A $600K decision should require more than a single click.

1
Copilot-drafted rationale
Pre-written text based on the AI analysis. Supervisor reviews, edits if needed, and submits.
2
Document request flow
If something is missing, a categorized checklist lets the supervisor select required documents. Agent is notified instantly.

Design Decisions

The choices that
define the product.

Every non-obvious decision in the Hub has a reason. Here are the four that shaped the experience most.

Notification type
Modal interrupt, not a toast
A snackbar says "FYI." A modal says "act now." With a 3-minute SLA and irreversible consequences, the interface must match the urgency. Supervisors cannot miss this.
Modal interruptToastBadge only
Decision entry point
Always a full drilldown
Inline approve buttons in the table would be faster - but speed isn't the only goal. Confidence and compliance are. Every decision requires full context review before action is possible.
Full case drilldownInline table actions
Rationale requirement
Mandatory - Copilot-assisted
Requiring a rationale creates intentional friction. The Copilot drafts it to reduce the burden - supervisors review and submit rather than write from scratch. Every decision is defensible.
Mandatory + Copilot draftOptional noteNo documentation
Missing documents
Structured checklist, not free text
When documents are missing, the supervisor picks from a categorized list. Structured requests are trackable, reduce agent confusion, and feed cleanly into the audit trail and case history.
Categorized checklistFree text message
