The User

The person holding the line
when the AI can't.

A contact center supervisor runs 8-15 agents at once. They're coaching mid-call, watching queue health, chasing metrics, handling escalations - all simultaneously, every shift.

And somewhere in that chaos, a $600K life insurance application is waiting for their sign-off. A real customer is on hold. The agent flagged it - correctly - as a case that requires human authorization.

Before Supervisor Hub, that handoff had nowhere to land properly.

The Flow

One decision point.
Three possible outcomes.
Every path audited.

When the AI agent flags a transaction that requires human authorization, the supervisor enters the flow. What follows is a precise sequence - designed so every decision is fast, informed, and defensible.

Screen 01 · Dashboard

Every urgent decision
surfaces before anything else.

The supervisor opens the Hub and the most critical item is already at the top. Not buried in a list - front and center, with an SLA clock running. Everything else - team status, performance, history - is one scroll away.

1
Stats row
Pending approvals, agents on shift, CSAT, and average handle time - always visible.
2
Authorization table
All requests sorted by urgency. SLA status, risk score, policy amount, and a Review button per row.
3
Copilot always-on

Screen 02 · Case Drilldown

The AI assembled the context.
The supervisor makes the call.

By the time the supervisor opens a case, the AI has already done the prep work: policy details pulled, compliance checks run, comparable cases surfaced, and a rationale drafted. The supervisor reads, evaluates, and decides - not searches.

Every decision requires a written rationale. Copilot drafts it. The supervisor owns it. That friction is intentional - a $600K decision deserves more than a tap.

1
AI Copilot analysis
What passed, what flagged, comparable approval rates - precomputed. No system-switching required.
2
Mandatory rationale
Copilot drafts it. Supervisor reviews and submits. Every decision is documented and defensible.
3
Document checklist
If something's missing, a categorized list lets the supervisor request specific documents. Agent is notified instantly.
