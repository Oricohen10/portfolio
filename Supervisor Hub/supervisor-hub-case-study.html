Context

A supervisor's shift never stops. Neither does the pressure.

A contact center supervisor manages 8-15 agents simultaneously. They're coaching, monitoring queues, handling escalations, reviewing performance - all in real time.

Verint's Agentic AI handles thousands of interactions autonomously. Most it resolves on its own. But when a decision crosses a threshold - legal exposure, policy limits, compliance flags - a human must be in the loop.

That human is the supervisor. And until now, there was no dedicated place for that moment to land.

The Problem

The AI can decide a lot.
But not everything. And that gap is expensive.

When an authorization request arrived, supervisors were expected to act - but they had no dedicated tool, no context, and no way to document their decision compliantly.

🔔
No dedicated interrupt
Requests arrived as generic notifications buried among other alerts. High-stakes decisions competed for attention with routine updates.
🧩
No context at the moment of decision
The supervisor had to navigate multiple systems to gather customer info, risk scores, and policy details before they could make a call.
📋
No audit trail
Decisions were made verbally or via chat. No structured rationale. No timestamped log. No compliance-ready documentation.

Screen 02 · Interrupt Modal

Not a notification. An interruption.

When an authorization request arrives, a full modal takes over the screen. It cannot be dismissed without acknowledgment. This is a deliberate design choice - a $600K decision deserves full attention.

1
SLA countdown bar
A visual timer shows how much time remains. The bar depletes in real time - urgency is visceral, not just text.
2
Key case details upfront
Customer name, policy amount, risk score, and agent - everything the supervisor needs to decide whether to act now or delegate.
3
Single primary action
"Review Case Now" is the only highlighted action. Dismiss is secondary - because dismissal should be a deliberate choice.
Screenshot: Interrupt Modal The red modal mid-screen with SLA bar and case summary
Screen 03 · Case Drilldown

Everything needed to decide. Nothing else.

The case view is focused on one thing: giving the supervisor enough context to make a confident decision in under 3 minutes. Policy details, compliance checks, AI analysis - all in one place.

1
AI Copilot analysis
What passed, what flagged, and comparable approval rates - precomputed so the supervisor doesn't have to research.
2
Copilot-drafted rationale
When the supervisor clicks Approve or Reject, the Copilot has already written the rationale. They review, edit if needed, and submit.
3
Document request flow
If something's missing, the supervisor selects from a categorized checklist. The agent is notified instantly.
Screenshot: Case Drilldown Case detail view with AI analysis and decision aside panel
Screen 04 · Copilot Rationale

The supervisor decides.
The Copilot explains why.

Every Approve or Reject requires a rationale. The Copilot prepares a draft based on the AI analysis - pre-written, accurate, and editable. The supervisor owns the decision; the Copilot reduces the cognitive load of documenting it.

This is not optional. The friction is intentional. A $600K decision should require more than a single click.

Design Decisions

The choices that define
the product.

Every non-obvious design decision in the Hub has a reason. Here are the ones that mattered most.

Notification type
Modal interrupt, not a toast
A snackbar says "FYI." A modal says "act now." With a 3-minute SLA and irreversible consequences, the UI must match the urgency. Supervisors cannot miss this.
Modal interrupt Toast / snackbar Badge only
Decision entry point
Always full drilldown
Inline approve/reject buttons in the table would be faster - but speed isn't the goal. Confidence and compliance are. Every decision requires full context review.
Full case drilldown Inline table actions
Rationale
Mandatory, Copilot-assisted
Requiring a rationale creates intentional friction. Copilot drafts it to reduce the burden - supervisors review and submit rather than write from scratch.
Mandatory + Copilot draft Optional note No documentation
Missing documents flow
Categorized checklist, not free text
When docs are missing, the supervisor picks from a structured list. This creates trackable requests, reduces agent confusion, and feeds cleanly into the audit trail.
Structured checklist Free text message
